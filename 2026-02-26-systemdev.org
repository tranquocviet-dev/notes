** Introduction to EDA
- a method of data analysis to get the main characteristics
- a graphical representation of data to provide insight and understanding
- a.k.a learn something from the data given by condensing data
** Descriptive statistics
- collect, summarize and present data
*** Central Tendency
- where the 'typical' value lies
- a range that signifies data measurement median
*** Variability
- how "spread out" the data is
** Data summarization techniques
*** Frequency distribution
- the number of times a unique value appears
*** Percentiles and quartiles
- percentiles devide into 100 part, quartiles into 4 parts based on how big the value is
*** Interquartile range
- range between q1 and q3, basically the middle 2 parts of a quartile
- *also* known as middle 50% of the range in terms of how big the values are
*** Box plots for visual summarization
- graphical representation of dataset based on quartiles
- showing the spread of the dataset
** Data visualization
- the graphical representation of data using graphs, tables, charts, maps
- they help convey complex info and aid in exploring patterns, relationships, outliers within the dataset
*** Principles
**** Clarity and simplicity
- avoid complexity, use straightforward language
**** Accuracy and precision
- double-check data accuracy, use appropriate scales
**** Relevance to the audience
- customize visuals to convey insighty that resonate with the audience, allow them to understand what we are conveying
**** Consistency in design
- use consistent themes and design for the presentation for cohesion and professionalism
*** Choosing the right visualization
- bar chart for categorized data
- histograms for distribution
- line chart for trends over time
- scatter plots for relationships
*** Scaling and formatting
- annotations sparingly
- clearly label axes, data points and informative titles
- appropritate scales to avoid misinterpretation
- choose an aethetically pleasing colorscheme
*** Common pitfalls
- Misleading scales
- overcrowded visuals (simplify visuals instead of using too many)
- lack of context
- misintepretation of data
** Challenges with EDA
*** Missing data in set
- add dummy data to set in place of missing data or consider the value of missing data
*** Handling outliers
- identify and adress outliers, then transform or remove the outlier
*** Ensuring data quality
- implement data cleaning procedures, validate sources and address inconsistencies
*** Selecting relevant Variables
- consult subject  matter and leverage statistical techniques
