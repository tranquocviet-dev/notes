# Theres a formula for everything, even circles and shapes
You should be familiar with $"rad"$, or radian. This measurement represented the angle of a circle, and is most often accompanied by the symbol $pi$, or pi. A $pi "rad"$ is equal to 180 degrees - half of 360 degrees, the same as a circle circumference. The way one can calculate most things that has to do with circles has to do with that measurement; for instance, the area of a circle with radius $r$ is $pi r^2$, whereas its circumference is $2 pi r$, or $pi d$ if $d$ is the diameter of the circle itself.

However, there are more complicated formulas for calculating trigonometry as well. The length of an arc, for example in the image bellow, is $theta / 2pi times 2 pi r$ or in short, $r theta$. Moreover, the Sector of OAB (the area) is $1/2 r^2 theta$, the chord of AB (the outline of OAB) is $2r sin theta/2$, whereas the area minor segment cut off by AB is $1/2 r^2 (theta minus sin theta)$.
